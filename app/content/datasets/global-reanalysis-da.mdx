  HydroGlobe data is available in two resolutions:
    - <Link href='/data-catalog/global-reanalysis-da-5'>HydroGlobe-5km</Link>: 5km resolution with improved data assimilation for enhanced accuracy. Temporal extent 2003-2024
    - <Link href='/data-catalog/global-reanalysis-da-10'>HydroGlobe-10km</Link>: 10km resolution with a temporal extent 2003-2020

  Link to VEDA Data story pages:
    - A 10-km View of the Global Water Cycle

  Realistic estimates of water, energy, and carbon cycle variables are crucial for accurately understanding Earth system processes. HydroGlobe provides these estimates by integrating satellite observations with model simulations using data assimilation within NASA’s Land Information System (LIS) modeling framework.

  ## Dataset Accuracy
  The HydroGlobe-10km dataset has been evaluated against both remotely sensed and in situ observations to assess its accuracy. Detailed reference datasets and evaluation results can be found in the Supporting Information section <a href="https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.2403707121" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">in this paper</a>. While HydroGlobe provides high-quality hydrological reanalysis data, users are strongly encouraged to verify that the dataset meets the accuracy requirements of their specific applications. The interpretation of results should be done with consideration of potential uncertainties inherent to the satellite observations utilized for data assimilation and the corresponding modeling processes.

  ## Scientific Details
  HydroGlobe integrates various remote-sensing datasets to provide real-world hydrological information. 

  Key Aspects of the Dataset:

  - The model: Both HydroGlobe-10km and HydroGlobe-5km use the Noah-MP land surface model within NASA LIS.
  - The meteorological forcings: 
    - HydroGlobe 5km is driven by meteorological fields from NASA's Modern-Era Retrospective analysis for Research and Applications version 2 (<a href="https://gmao.gsfc.nasa.gov/reanalysis/merra-2/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">MERRA-2</a>), radiation products from NASA’s Clouds and the Earth’s Radiant Energy System (<a href="https://ceres.larc.nasa.gov/#:~:text=What%20is%20CERES?,improvement%20through%20model%2Dobservation%20intercomparisons." target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">CERES</a>) project, and precipitation from Integrated Multi-satellitE Retrievals for Global Precipitation Measurement (<a href="https://gpm.nasa.gov/data/imerg" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">IMERG</a>) that has been downscaled and bias-corrected based on WorldClim precipitation climatology.
    - HydroGlobe 10km is driven by a combination of NASA’s Modern-Era Retrospective analysis for Research and Applications version 2 (<a href="https://gmao.gsfc.nasa.gov/reanalysis/merra-2/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">MERRA-2</a>) data and Integrated Multi-satellitE Retrievals for Global Precipitation Measurement (<a href="https://gpm.nasa.gov/data/imerg" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">IMERG</a>) rainfall data.
  - The assimilated remote-sensing datasets:
    - HydroGlobe-10km assimilated combined soil moisture product from European Space Agency (ESA CCI), leaf area index (LAI) from Moderate Resolution Imaging Spectroradiometer (<a href="https://modis.gsfc.nasa.gov/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">MODIS</a>), and terrestrial water storage anomalies from Gravity Recovery and Climate Experiment and follow-on satellites (GRACE/GRACE-FO).
    - In addition to the remote-sensing products that HydroGlobe-10km adopted, HydroGlobe-5km includes the assimilation of soil moisture from NASA SMAP for 2023-2024 when ESA CCI product is not available and the assimilation of snow water equivalent derived from MODIS using machine learning-based reconstruction approaches.
