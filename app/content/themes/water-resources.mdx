  ## About Water Availability
  The global water cycle is critical for providing freshwater for human consumption, powering electricity generation, sustaining agriculture through irrigation, and driving ecosystems. Reliable estimates of water availability is challenging because multiple processes interact with each other at both large and small temporal and spatial scales, like precipitation, soil moisture, groundwater, snow accumulation and melt. NASA Water Insight provides data from satellites and models on freshwater in near real time to understand these complicated and interconnected processes. 

  The Water Insight team is building the state-of-the-art North American Land Data Assimilation System Phase 3 (NLDAS-3) modeling system for improved estimates of water availability. NLDAS-3 combines the open source NASA Land Information System (LIS) with remotely sensed observations to provide daily estimates of land surface fluxes and states such as soil moisture, streamflow, and snow. The information from NLDAS-3 will be useful not only for water availability, but also for capturing and estimating hazards such as floods and droughts, both of which can be costly for agriculture and human infrastructure.
